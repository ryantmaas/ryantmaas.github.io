VFD Application Challenges

Variable frequency drive (VFD) cables carry power from AC drive systems to AC motors. As a result, these cables must handle not only overall high power signals, but also the extremely high voltage which can occur when standing waves develop on the conductors.

The main challenges of VFD applications include:

Noise / Common Mode Current (CMC)

Reflected Wave Voltage

System Reliability & Safety

Typical Cabling Solutions

Typical cabling solutions for this application have been unshielded tray cables, single-conductor THHN wire installed in conduit or continuously-welded armored cable.

Not only do these products suffer from complex, costly installation and potential reliability problems, but they also:

Have proven to be ineffective in resolving issues with radiated noise, voltage spikes and the service life of VFD systems

Degrade and fail in the in the face of voltage spikes and heat build-up

Do not protect against capacitive coupling (cable charging) , resulting in reduced motor torque, false motor trips and currents being induced onto other systems, introducing risk to human safety

Are not recommended by most drive manufacturers for use in VFD applications
